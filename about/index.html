About

The name of this blog is borrowed from the old motto of the Institute of Actuaries. While certain unfortunate realities have prompted the profession to abandon this paean to the central limit theorem, it remains that insurance applications have provided some of the foundational motivation for the development of modern statistics.

The version of the Institute of Actuaries crest in use since 1956 until 2010. The wavy lines on the arms and the crest are supposed to represent “mathematical analysis”!

I’m Dimitri Semenovich, an AI researcher and actuary working at the intersection of AI, causal inference, and insurance. Currently, I lead pricing, personalisation and AI at Cover Genius, a series D startup providing insurance solutions to customers of leading ecommerce platforms like Booking Holdings, Uber, and Amazon.

Previously, I spent six years at Insurance Australia Group, where I’ve developedreal-time machine learning models for risk pricing and worked on various AI and analytics initiatives.

Education & Credentials

  • PhD in Computer Science (Mathematical optimization in machine learning), UNSW
  • Fellow of the Institute of Actuaries of Australia (FIAA)

Research Interests

I’m particularly interested in:

  • Evaluation of AI systems
  • Applications of convex optimization in insurance pricing
  • Causal inference methods for business decision-making
  • Forecast verification and evaluation
  • Sequential analysis and experimental design

Professional Activities

I currently serve as a Board Member of the ASTIN Section of the International Actuarial Association and as Chair of its Scientific Committee. I’m also a Research Fellow at the Gradient Institute, working on bridging the gap between algorithmic/AI fairness and actuarial practice.

If you’re interested in chatting about any of these topics or potential collaborations, feel free to reach out at dvsemenovich@gmail.com.
